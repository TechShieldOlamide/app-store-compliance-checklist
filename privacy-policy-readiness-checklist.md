# Privacy Policy Readiness Checklist

> Created by [TrustGrid Technology Limited](https://trustgridhq.com) — App Security & Compliance Consulting.
> Use this checklist to verify your privacy policy meets GDPR, NDPR, and CCPA requirements before app submission.

---

## 1. Privacy Policy Basics

- [ ] Privacy policy exists and is publicly accessible
- [ ] Privacy policy accessible without requiring login
- [ ] Privacy policy URL added to App Store / Play Store listing
- [ ] Privacy policy link visible in app (settings, onboarding, or footer)
- [ ] Privacy policy written in clear, plain language
- [ ] Privacy policy identifies the data controller clearly
- [ ] Contact details for privacy enquiries provided
- [ ] Last updated date shown on privacy policy
- [ ] Version number maintained for audit purposes

---

## 2. Data Collection Disclosure

- [ ] All categories of personal data collected are listed
- [ ] Purpose for collecting each data category is explained
- [ ] Legal basis for processing each data category stated
- [ ] Third-party SDKs that collect data are disclosed
- [ ] Cookies and tracking technologies disclosed
- [ ] Device data collection disclosed (IP address, device ID, etc.)
- [ ] Biometric data collection disclosed if applicable
- [ ] Financial data collection disclosed if applicable
- [ ] Location data collection disclosed if applicable

---

## 3. GDPR Requirements (EU Users)

- [ ] Data controller identity and contact details provided
- [ ] DPO contact details provided if applicable
- [ ] All six lawful bases for processing covered where relevant
- [ ] Right of access explained and contact method provided
- [ ] Right to rectification explained
- [ ] Right to erasure (right to be forgotten) explained
- [ ] Right to restrict processing explained
- [ ] Right to data portability explained
- [ ] Right to object explained
- [ ] Rights related to automated decision-making explained
- [ ] Data retention periods specified for each data category
- [ ] International data transfer safeguards explained
- [ ] Right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority included
- [ ] EU representative details if no EU establishment

---

## 4. NDPR Requirements (Nigerian Users)

- [ ] Data controller identity clearly stated
- [ ] Purpose of data collection stated
- [ ] Categories of data collected listed
- [ ] Data subject rights explained
- [ ] Right to access personal data
- [ ] Right to rectification
- [ ] Right to deletion
- [ ] Right to object to processing
- [ ] Data retention periods specified
- [ ] Third-party sharing disclosed
- [ ] Cross-border transfer information provided
- [ ] NDPC complaint right mentioned
- [ ] Data Protection Officer details if applicable

---

## 5. CCPA Requirements (California Users)

- [ ] Categories of personal information collected disclosed
- [ ] Purposes for collection disclosed
- [ ] Categories of third parties data is shared with disclosed
- [ ] Right to know about personal information collected explained
- [ ] Right to delete personal information explained
- [ ] Right to opt-out of sale of personal information explained
- [ ] Right to non-discrimination for exercising rights explained
- [ ] "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link if data is sold
- [ ] Designated method for submitting consumer requests provided
- [ ] Response timeline for consumer requests stated (45 days)

---

## 6. Children's Privacy

- [ ] Age restriction clearly stated if app is not for children
- [ ] COPPA compliance section included if app may be used by under 13s
- [ ] GDPR Article 8 compliance if EU children may use app
- [ ] Parental consent mechanism described if applicable
- [ ] No behavioural advertising to children

---

## 7. Data Security

- [ ] Security measures described in general terms
- [ ] Encryption of data in transit mentioned
- [ ] Encryption of data at rest mentioned
- [ ] Access controls described
- [ ] Data breach notification process described
- [ ] Breach notification timeline stated (72 hours for GDPR/NDPR)

---

## 8. Data Retention

- [ ] Retention periods stated for each data category
- [ ] Criteria used to determine retention periods explained
- [ ] Deletion process described
- [ ] Legal hold exceptions noted

---

## 9. Third-Party Sharing & Processors

- [ ] All third-party data processors listed or categorised
- [ ] Purpose of sharing with each third party explained
- [ ] Data Processing Agreements in place with all processors
- [ ] Third-party links/services disclaimed

---

## 10. User Rights & Contact

- [ ] Clear process for submitting data subject requests
- [ ] Response timeline stated (30 days standard)
- [ ] Email address for privacy requests provided and monitored
- [ ] Escalation path to DPO or supervisory authority provided
- [ ] Process for withdrawing consent described

## Common Rejection Reasons

These are the most common reasons apps get rejected or flagged for privacy policy issues:

1. Privacy policy URL is broken or returns 404
2. Privacy policy does not match data declared in App Store privacy label
3. Privacy policy does not cover all third-party SDKs
4. No data subject rights section
5. No contact details for privacy enquiries
6. Privacy policy behind login wall
